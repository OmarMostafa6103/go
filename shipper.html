How GoLynk Works for Your Shipments

1

Define What Moves

You tell us what needs to move: parcels, boxes, pallets, LTL or FTL. You set origins, destinations, time windows, and service levels (same‑day, next‑day, 2–3 days).

2

We Orchestrate the Route

GoLynk's engine builds a multi‑hop, multi‑modal route. For parcels, it combines walkers, bikes, vans, trains, and microhubs. For pallets and LTL, it taps underutilized trucks, regional carriers, and backhauls on your lanes.

3

You Get Delivery and Data

Shipments arrive within the agreed window, with one view of events, costs, and CO₂ per shipment. You get a single invoice and exportable data for finance and ESG.

GoLynk Technology

The orchestration engine behind every route.

Orchestration Graph

We model routes from individuals, fleets, and freight partners as one graph of movements and capacities—across walkers, bikes, cars, vans, trucks, trains, and public transport. This lets us see the free space already in motion, instead of always booking new vehicles.

Matching Engine

A rule‑based engine scores each potential leg by route overlap, time window, capacity, detour cost, reliability, and carbon impact. It then chains 2–3 hops into one optimal journey per shipment, so parcels and pallets can flow across several carriers and modes without extra journeys.

API‑First Integration

Your TMS, WMS, e‑commerce and ERP plug into GoLynk via APIs and dashboards. You expose underused capacity, integrate tracking, and bring cost and CO₂ data back into your own systems—all on top of the carriers and lanes you already use.

Data‑Driven Roadmap

We start with robust rule‑based matching and real‑time tracking. As data grows, machine learning quietly improves match rates, ETAs, and pricing over time—without adding complexity for your teams or changing how you book shipments.
